About

Krishane Patel is a leading behavioural scientist, with a particular interest on decision-making and behaviour. Krishane read Psychology at the University of Warwick and decided to embark on a career in behavioural sciences. Krishane pursued a MRes in Psychological Research Methods from the University of York, where he worked under the world renowned psychologist Prof. Alan Baddeley. After completing his Masters, Krishane sought after a Research Assistant (voluntary) at Imperial College London under Prof Ivo Vlaev and Prof Lord Ara Darzi working on behavioural science and decision-making research in contexts of health, finance and politics.


Krishane was offered an ESRC award to pursue doctoral training at one of Europe's leading business schools, Warwick Business School, in the behavioural science group. Krishane led and managed a large-scale randomised controlled trial and was able to instill long-lasting collaborations across Cambridge and Monash University. Krishane's thesis evaluated behavioural interventions to reduce monetary consumption in university students using goal-setting (asking participants to set financial goals to save towards) or behavioural substitutions (swapping a daily coffee for a tea). His thesis was awarded spin-off funding to launch a larger investigation with adults in social housing. 


Krishane was awarded a presitigous position applying behavioural science in public policy settings as a joint position between University College London's Centre for Behaviour Change the Food Standards Agency. Krishane led the experimental and research work in the team, consulting and advising on research design, the social science team on aspects of behavioural science practice and research methods. During this time her worked on various research projects involving regulatory practice, allergen, communications, and supply chains. After which, he worked as a Postdoctoral Researcher at Warwick Manufacturing Group at the University of Warwick. In this role Krishane led the data management and analysis for clinical trials to improve insomnia and mental health in the workplace.


Currently, Krishane is a Specialist Consultant working at NatWest Banking Group, applying behavioural science to help support the Bank achieve its strategic aims in the context of Consumer Duty.
