Background

Partnering with a media agency to launch a new financial product. A IFISA product, is a financial product that offers better reward than a bank savings account but way less risk than say throwing your money onto the stock markets. To this end, the agency wanted to identify Britain's most and least risky town/city, and to then identify Britain's most middle tow/city when it comes to risk. The middle town representing the dichotomy of risk and reward conceptualised by the IFISA product.

Approach

Data gathered from Google Trends for the two dimensions (risk and safety) was collected at the city-level. Risk was conceptualised as terms such as "gambling","danger" and "car accident" whilst safety was characterised with terms such as "protection", "vaccination" and "seatbelt". A safety index was computed as the fraction of risk by safety. Data was analysed to identify the city in the middle of the risk distribution.

Outcome

Watford was highlighted as the middle amount of risk, where the risk index showed that overall risk was proximal to safety. The most riskiest cities were shown to be Burnham, whilst the least riskiest was Exeter.
